LINUX FOR THE MINIMALIST PART 3 : THE PROCESS OF GOING MINIMAL - SEPT 9TH 2021



  • The first way: Gradual shedding.

  • The second way: Transition.

  • The Third way: Cold Turkey.



This is the part where there is this question "where do I begin to do so-and-so?" in most how-to books. Well this is no different.

The process of minimizing the OS to a barebones yet useful system shares a basic philosophy with other forms of minimalism; A delicate balance of simplicity and practicality. It is not just as simple as saying "to hell with XYZ app", but rather "how can I remedy the problems that XYZ app provided and simplify the matter".

There are three ways to go about this;



METHOD I: Gradual shedding.

When one installs a distro of Linux these days, you will find that there are a ton of apps and features. What is amazing, is that one can uninstall many of these features and apps without "breaking" the operating system.

And so, as you use a Linux distribution, you can gradually uninstall the stuff you don't use or want. You can also begin replacing certain apps with apps that are simpler and fit your needs.

When you boil it down to the bare necessities, a browser, some text editor and a file manager is all you really need. Any extras app you install, is up to the needs of the user.



METHOD II: Transition.

Whereas the gradual shedding of unnecessary/unwanted software is a slow process. Transitioning from a complex desktop to a more streamlined version takes a little more effort and often takes some "re-learning" of an environment.

For example, the whole idea of Windows Managers. At the core, Linux has XORG and then it has the Windows Managers. There are several ones that carry a very small footprint but also bring a whole new level of minimalism that supersedes the gradual shedding of unwanted apps.

These, will be explored in a later chapter, along some suggestions of how to configure it so that it may be useful.



METHOD III: Cold Turkey.

Just like some quit smoking, this one requires one to go with a clean install of a barebones linux CLI. From there one can carefully install the software that is wanted from scratch.

This methods will be discussed further in a later chapter, step by step.
